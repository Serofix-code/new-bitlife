Getting started

Create a user account, then create a character. Choose a country, identity, reproductive role, occult type, special talent, upbringing, portrait, and optional story seed. A story seed makes random generation repeatable for testing.

Press Age to move forward one year. Most systems use age, health, happiness, smarts, resilience, money, legal status, talents, and past choices when calculating outcomes.

Life cycle and age progression

Childhood

Ages 0–11 focus on family, school, automatic milestones, simple wellbeing activities, and age-up events.

Teen years

Ages 12–17 unlock clubs, social media, lessons, licences, part-time work, and additional relationship options.

Adulthood

At age 18, full careers, assets, relocation, fertility, adoption, major crime, legal claims, and expansions unlock.

Death and generations

When a life ends, a ribbon is calculated from the character’s history. A living child can inherit the estate and continue the family line. Ribbons and one-time challenges are stored on the user account, not only on one generation.

Dynamic Activities

Activities are not simple fixed buttons. Every option has requirements and an estimated result chance. The game checks character variables before deciding the outcome.

  • Health & Mind: doctors, exercise, meditation, therapy, libraries, and diet plans.
  • Social Media: posts, videos, livestreams, collaborations, followers, and campaigns.
  • Clubs & Teams: drama, sports, music, debate, and volunteering.
  • Licences: driving, boating, and pilot tests.
  • Civil Law: consultations and fictional disputes.
  • Vacations: local, domestic, international, and luxury trips.
  • Leisure: cinema, nightlife, fame actions, pets, and appearance choices.

Most actions consume one activity point. Age up to receive new points. Prison replaces outside activities with jail-only options.

Fertility, adoption, and relationships

Family-building unlocks in adulthood and uses a 0–100 biological success metric. Age, health, reproductive role, partner compatibility, contraception, medical routes, finances, and legal history affect the result.

  • Natural attempts require a compatible partner.
  • Artificial insemination and IVF use medical success modifiers.
  • Surrogacy can include delays or contract complications.
  • Adoption checks income, stability, housing, adulthood, and criminal history.

Successful routes create a pending family process. The child arrives through an age-up event rather than instantly. Relationships track identity, pronouns, age, closeness, marriage, divorce, exes, late partners, and inheritance.

Occults

Vampires

Vampires do not die from old age. They still interact with health, danger, relationships, secrecy, and other life systems.

Wizards, witches, and mages

Magic can be selected at birth, inherited, or awakened later. Wizards have a longer average lifespan and can cast spells using mana and power. Spells include age changes, restoration, fortune, granting magic, charms, and dangerous fictional magic with legal consequences.

Occult traits can pass to children and playable heirs.

Crime, court, prison, and fugitives

Crime success and police detection use chance-based calculations. Arrests create court cases with lawyer options. Convictions create persistent sentences and criminal records that reduce job and adoption chances.

While incarcerated, jobs, assets, outside relationships, ordinary activities, and expansions are locked. Jail activities include exercise, reading, prison work, calls, parole, appeals, and an abstract escape attempt. Escaped prisoners have a normal annual recapture risk that rises with fame and criminal history.

Money and assets

Money is stored in a common internal value and displayed using the current country’s currency conversion. Relocating changes the displayed currency and local market.

Generated shopping listings include homes, vehicles, bicycles, jewellery, and instruments. Items have pictures, prices, condition, upkeep, and resale values. Real estate generally holds value better than vehicles.

Investment Expansion

The first expansion adds a fictional stock market. Each life receives twelve randomly generated companies with names, sectors, prices, risk levels, volatility, dividend yields, and histories.

  • Trading unlocks at age 18.
  • Company research unlocks at age 16 and consumes an activity point.
  • Buy and sell any whole number of shares.
  • Small trading fees apply unless disabled by a developer cheat.
  • Prices change whenever the character ages up.
  • Companies can experience strong years, weak years, or major fictional setbacks.
  • Dividend-paying companies may add cash each year.
  • The portfolio tracks average cost, current value, unrealized result, realized result, dividends, and trade history.

The companies and market are completely fictional and the expansion is a game mechanic, not financial advice.

Accounts, saves, challenges, and ribbons

Multiple local users can exist in the same browser. Each user has separate autosaves, named saves, ribbons, and one-time challenge progress.

Ribbons are awarded when a character dies and saved permanently to that user. Challenges can only award completion once per user. Exported JSON saves can be moved between devices. The included home server can share one save between devices on the same network.

Developer menu

The Developer menu contains testing tools for age, money, stats, events, relationships, crime, jail, careers, occults, fertility, activities, ribbons, challenges, and investments.

Investment cheats

  • Add investment cash.
  • Add shares of every fictional company.
  • Force a market boom or crash.
  • Make future market years profitable.
  • Disable trading fees.
  • Reveal company outlooks.
  • Max or clear the portfolio.

Developer actions are intended for private customization and testing and can drastically change save balance.
