Getting started

Create a local user account, then create a character. Choose a country, identity, reproductive role, occult type, special talent, upbringing, portrait, and optional story seed.

Press Age to move forward one year. Age, health, happiness, smarts, resilience, money, legal status, talents, followers, fame, housing, and previous choices can all affect results.

Life cycle and compact age-up updates

The Life Journal stores major choices and smaller yearly headlines. Compact updates can report a sibling graduating kindergarten, a child taking first steps, a relative starting a career, an investment rally, a crypto crash, business income, landlord repairs, rent payments, audience growth, or a fame award.

Childhood

Ages 0–11 focus on family, school, automatic milestones, simple wellbeing activities, and age-up events.

Teen years

Ages 12–17 unlock clubs, social media, lessons, licences, part-time work, and additional relationship options.

Adulthood

At age 18, full careers, assets, relocation, fertility, adoption, major crime, legal claims, and all installed expansions unlock.

Death and generations

When a life ends, a ribbon is calculated from the character’s history. A living child can inherit the estate and continue the family line. Financial investments, crypto holdings, businesses, and landlord properties can pass to the next generation.

Dynamic Activities

Activities are variable-driven systems rather than fixed buttons. Every option has requirements and an estimated result chance.

  • Health & Mind: doctors, exercise, meditation, therapy, libraries, and diet plans.
  • Social Media: posts, videos, livestreams, photo stories, podcasts, collaborations, community events, and sponsorships.
  • Clubs & Teams: drama, sports, music, debate, and volunteering.
  • Licences: driving, boating, and pilot tests.
  • Civil Law: consultations and fictional disputes.
  • Vacations: local, domestic, international, and luxury trips.
  • Leisure: cinema, nightlife, fame actions, pets, and appearance choices.

Most actions consume one activity point. Prison replaces outside activities and expansion management with jail-only options.

Social media and fame

Social media

The creator system tracks followers, engagement, reputation, posting streaks, creator level, monetization, lifetime creator income, and the largest follower gain from one post.

Successful posts can grow fame and unlock livestreams, collaborations, fan community events, and premium sponsorships. Inactive accounts can lose momentum during age progression.

Fame

Fame tracks public image, press mentions, awards, endorsements, and fan events. Public actions include interviews, memoirs, commercials, photoshoots, charity appearances, red-carpet events, fan meetings, merchandise, and documentaries.

Fame can gradually decline if the character stops appearing publicly, unless a developer control disables decay.

Fertility, adoption, and relationships

Family-building unlocks in adulthood and uses a 0–100 biological success metric. Age, health, reproductive role, partner compatibility, contraception, medical routes, finances, and legal history affect the result.

  • Natural attempts require a compatible partner.
  • Artificial insemination and IVF use medical success modifiers.
  • Surrogacy can include delays or contract complications.
  • Adoption checks income, stability, housing, adulthood, and criminal history.

Successful routes create a pending family process. The child arrives through age progression rather than instantly. Relationship milestones can later appear as compact Life Journal updates.

Occults

Vampires

Vampires do not die from old age. They still interact with health, danger, relationships, secrecy, and other life systems.

Wizards, witches, and mages

Magic can be selected at birth, inherited, or awakened later. Magic includes age changes, restoration, fortune, granting magic, charms, and dangerous fictional spells with legal consequences.

Crime, court, prison, and fugitives

Crime success and police detection use chance-based calculations. Arrests create court cases with lawyer options. Convictions create persistent sentences and criminal records that reduce job and adoption chances.

While incarcerated, jobs, assets, ordinary activities, trading, business management, and landlord management are locked. Existing investments and properties can still change value while the character is in custody.

Money and assets

Money is stored in a common internal value and displayed using the current country’s currency. Relocating changes the displayed currency and local market.

Generated shopping listings include homes, vehicles, bicycles, jewellery, and instruments. Net worth also includes investments, crypto holdings, businesses, and landlord property values.

Investment Expansion

Each life receives twelve randomly generated fictional companies with sectors, prices, risk, volatility, dividends, histories, and line-chart previews.

  • Research unlocks at age 16 and trading at age 18.
  • Buy and sell whole shares.
  • Track cost basis, current value, realized results, unrealized results, dividends, and transactions.
  • Prices update whenever the character ages up.
  • Large movements can appear as compact Life Journal headlines.

The market is fictional and is not financial advice.

Crypto Expansion

Each life receives eight randomly generated fictional digital assets. Their prices can change far more sharply than company shares.

  • Trading unlocks at age 18.
  • Fractional units can be bought and sold.
  • Trading fees, cost basis, holdings, realized results, and transactions are tracked.
  • Fictional confidence crises and sudden attention can cause crashes or moonshots.
  • Every asset has a line-chart preview and annual history.

All names, prices, and market events are fictional.

Business Expansion

Characters can start and own up to eight companies. Available types include cafés, online stores, creative studios, salons, fitness centres, logistics companies, technology startups, and boutique hotels.

Businesses track employees, quality, advertising, reputation, revenue, expenses, profit, value, and annual history. Owners can invest, advertise, hire staff, or sell. Profits add cash, while losses can create debt.

Renting & Landlord Expansion

Renting a home

Adult characters can rent a shared room, studio, apartment, family house, or luxury penthouse. Leases include a deposit and yearly rent. Ending a lease returns part of the deposit.

Landlord properties

Characters can purchase rental flats, apartments, family homes, duplexes, and small apartment blocks. Each property tracks tenants, vacancy, monthly rent, maintenance, condition, appreciation, value, and annual net income.

Landlords can find tenants, renovate, or sell properties. Rental properties contribute to net worth and inheritance.

Accounts, saves, challenges, and ribbons

Multiple local users can exist in the same browser. Each user has separate autosaves, named saves, ribbons, and one-time challenge progress.

Exported JSON saves can be moved between devices. The included home server can share one save between devices on the same local network.

Countries and cities

Lives can begin in twenty-one countries, each with localized names, currencies, flags, and city pools. Emigration and travel use the same expanded world catalog.

Developer menu

The Developer menu contains testing controls for age, money, stats, events, relationships, crime, jail, careers, occults, fertility, activities, ribbons, challenges, investments, crypto, businesses, landlords, social media, and fame.

Expansion controls

  • Force investment or crypto booms and crashes.
  • Disable investment or crypto trading fees.
  • Add shares or fictional crypto holdings.
  • Make businesses always profitable.
  • Keep landlord properties occupied.
  • Make social posts go viral.
  • Prevent fame decay.
  • Max or clear expansion systems.

Developer actions can drastically change save balance.
